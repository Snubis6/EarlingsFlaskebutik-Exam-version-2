Beer types


Everybody knows what beer is, it’s hard not to, if you passed 18.
But do we actually know it? Probably not fully.
We would like to help our customers with this small bit in here,
so you can be sure next time what kind of beers you like
or what you haven’t tried yet!

The two sections down will be about lagers and ales.
Open them up and read the small description about the styles they have,
so next time, when you shop at our place you won’t feel lost!

Lagers


Lager, coming from the German word Lagerbier referred to the
bottom-fermented, cool-conditioned beer. It is considered
as a great launching pad for newcomers, as it doesn’t have
as bitter taste as the ales. Usually known for its pale golden colour
, but lagers also have darker types too.

Most famous lagers:

Most famous lagers:

  • - Carlsberg (Denmark)
  • - Carling (UK)
  • - Pilsner Urquell (Czech Republic)
  • - Tsingtao (China)

Pilsner


The most famous one among the lagers, this style came from a city called Plzen in the Czech Republic.
Color: From light straw to pale golden
Taste: Crisp, the German versions can be a bit bitter
Fun fact: Most of the American lagers are based on pilsners.

Famous pilsners:

  • - Heineken
  • - Pilsner Urquell (first pilsner ever made)
  • - Stella Artois
  • - Budweiser Budvar

Helles/Hell


Color: Light colored golden
Taste: Mildly sweet, not that bitter, less hop flavor than pilsner
Fun fact: It is often referred to as Munich lager, and because of its light taste it compliments salads and seefood.

Famous Hells:

  • - Löwenbräu
  • - Penn Brewery Gold
  • - Hofbräu München>

Märzen/Märzenbier


Color: From pale to amber and even dark brown
Taste: Sweet, less hoppy flavour
Fun fact: It is the traditional beer of the Octoberfest.

Famous Märzens:

  • - Sierra Nevada Octoberfest
  • - Heater Allen Bobtoberfest
  • - Schilling Konstantin

Vienna lager


Color: Medium amber to reddish-brown
Taste: Not that bitter and hoppy, more malty sweet
Fun fact: Visiting England’s big brewing cities also helped a lot to make the first Vienna lager.

Famous Vienna lagers:

  • - 100 Blumen
  • - Mutter Milch
  • - Loncium

Bock


Color: Dark, from light copper to brown
Taste: Strong (all types has high alcohol content % (6.3 – 14), malty, toasty
Fun fact: It has 4 styles next to the traditional, Maibock, Doppelbock, Eisbock and Weizenbock

Famous Bocks:

  • - Weihenstephaner Korbinian
  • - Saint Arnold Spring Bock
  • - Ayinger Celebrator

Dunkel/Dunkles


Color: From dark amber to dark brown
Taste: Smooth malty
Fun fact: If you order Dunkel in pubs, sometimes it can just mean that you’ll get whatever dark lagers they have.

Famous Dunkels:

  • - Hofbräu München Dunkel
  • - König Ludvig Dunkel
  • - Dunkel Fester

Schwarzbier


Color: Dark, opaque black
Taste: Chocolaty, coffee-ish, strong
Fun fact: It taste like Stout (Ale) beers, it’s because it was traditionally made as an ale, but they later tried cool fermentation instead of warm.

Famous Scwarzbier:

  • - Köstritzer
  • - Samuel Adams Black Lager
  • - Xingu Black

Ales


The warm fermented ale is a type of beer that referred to a drink without hops.
Most of them has a stronger bitter taste, and with that a usually stronger
and darker color, compared to the lagers. The name “ale”
(or if you spell it as øl) in different languages can mean just beer itself,
as in Danish too.

Most famous ales:

  • - Royal (Denmark)
  • - Guiness Dry Stout (Ireland)
  • - Dogfish Head 90 Minute IPA (United States)
  • - Sierra Nevada Pale Ale (United States)

Pale Ale


Color: Golden to amber
Taste: Fruity, crisp, overpowering hoppy flavor
Fun fact: There are more than 10 types of Pale ale families in the world, the most famous is the India Pale Ale (IPA) and the American Pale Ale (APA)

Famous Pale ales:

  • - AleSmith X Extra Pale Ale
  • - Boréale Sur Pilotis
  • - Sudden Death Spaceman Dust

American Pale Ale (APA)


Color: Dark amber
Taste: Hop taste with malt sweetness, hint of citrus, pine or flowers
Fun fact: Made in America, made for American food. Recommended to drink next to grilled steak, burgers, fried chicken.

Famous APAs:

  • - Three Floyds Zombie Dust
  • - Three Floyds Alpha King
  • - Lagunitas Born Yesterday Pale Ale

India Pale Ale (IPA)


Color: Light and dark amber
Taste: Depends on brewery, can be citrusy or just strong and bitter
Fun fact: The name is tricky, it wasn’t invented in India, it was just the beer that was good enough to survive the road to India during colonization.

Famous IPA:

  • - AleSmith IPA
  • - Maine Beer Lunch
  • - Bell’s Two Hearted Ale

Porter


Color: Dark brown
Taste: Acidic, dry, sweet, moderately bitter
Fun fact: Porter was the original dark beer, as stout was called “Strong/stout porter” back in the days

Famous Porters:

  • - Hill Farmstead Everett
  • - Founders Porter
  • - Mikkeller Porter

Stout


Color: Dark brown
Taste: Strong, roasted coffee/chocolate, caramel sweetness
Fun fact: There are a lot of variations, like oatmeal stout and milk stout.

Famous Stouts:

  • - Guinesse
  • - Half Acre Original Reaper
  • - Stone Xocoveza Stout
