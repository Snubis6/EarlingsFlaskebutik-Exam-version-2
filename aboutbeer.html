Beer types


Everybody knows what beer is, it’s hard not to, if you passed 18.
But do we actually know it? Probably not fully.
We would like to help our customers with this small bit in here,
so you can be sure next time what kind of beers you like
or what you haven’t tried yet!

The two sections down will be about lagers and ales.
Open them up and read the small description about the styles they have,
so next time, when you shop at our place you won’t feel lost!

Lager

Lager, coming from the German word Lagerbier referred to the
bottom-fermented, cool-conditioned beer. It is considered
as a great launching pad for newcomers, as it doesn’t have
as bitter taste as the ales. Usually known for its pale golden colour
, but lagers also have darker types too.

Most famous lagers:

  • Carlsberg (Denmark)
  • Carling (UK)
  • Pilsner Urquell (Czech Republic)
  • Tsingtao (China)

Ale

The warm fermented ale is a type of beer that referred to a drink without hops.
Most of them has a stronger bitter taste, and with that a usually stronger
and darker color, compared to the lagers. The name “ale”
(or if you spell it as øl) in different languages can mean just beer itself,
as in Danish too.

Most famous ales:

  • Royal (Denmark)
  • Guiness Dry Stout (Ireland)
  • Dogfish Head 90 Minute IPA (United States)
  • Sierra Nevada Pale Ale (United States)
